# manifest

## Scope

The argument's organization presents three paths: twelve scenes divided between student and designer, and twelve nexus pages that explore theoretical and pedagogical consequences of what the scenes narrate. Beyond the init welcome page, the webtext's frame presents six pages of orientation and apparatus, from interpretive environment to specification. A companion evidence graph publishes the course design as inspectable documentation. The webtext totals thirty primary content pages plus the external graph.

## What This Is Not

Some nodes in this webtext depart from customarily academic veridical modes. Two characters, a course designer and a student, explore the experience of a constraint-sequenced semester. The narration of their scenes avoids the confessional mode of teaching reflections and seeks to investigate pedagogical relations at sites of contestation.

The economics are not metaphorical. When this webtext describes student writing as capital accumulation or course design as labor allocation, it argues for those terms literally. The argument claims that composition already operates as economic activity, and that naming such operations opens under-examined pedagogical and theoretical possibilities.

This is not an empirical study. There are no IRB approvals or historical classroom data. The evidence is structural. The webtext offers a complete course design published as an associatively explorable evidence graph and available for inspection at a scale that exceeds what conventional appendices permit.

What this webtext offers, then, is a networked argument about design whose method is its medium: a constraint structure theorized, narrated, and enacted as navigational experience good. It asks you to observe yourself in the interconnected cycle of reading, navigating, and meaning-making.

### `/track/grant/`

Grant Miles works the circulation desk at the university library and is enrolled in a section of first-year writing. His scenes follow the semester's doing-order: what it feels like to encounter a constraint-sequenced course.

### `/track/vera/`

Vera Green designs the course's constraint sequence. Her scenes follow the designer's doing-order: what it feels like to build a course that decomposes writing into discrete operations, and then to help students work through it.

### `/tree/`

Twelve pages of academic discussion develop the webtext's theoretical and pedagogical claims. Each page advances a node in the argument; together they compose multiple understanding-orders in which the webtext's reasoning accumulates. These pages first become accessible through the narrative tracks.

### `/graph/`

The webtext publishes the course design that the scenes narrate and the nexus pages theorize as a companion evidence graph. Student-facing materials, assessment architecture, constraint sequences, and weekly schedules are available for inspection in a navigable network.

### `/frame/`

Six pages stage the argument by setting the interpretive environment, providing direction, documenting infrastructure and the webtext's development. The tracks narrate; the trees theorize; the graph documents. The frame supports, positions, and directs attention.
