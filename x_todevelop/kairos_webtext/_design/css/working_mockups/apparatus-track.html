This page documents the tools, design decisions, and infrastructure behind Constraint Structure: Toward an Economy of Composition. The course design this article documents argues that making process visible is itself pedagogically productive. This specification practices that argument: the apparatus is part of the work.

design_system

Visual Design

The webtext uses an Editorial Brutalist aesthetic: heavy borders, substantial typographic weight, flat color, sharp corners. The style communicates historical situatedness rather than timelessness. Design decisions are intentional and visible rather than naturalized. Four distinct color palettes and typographic voices differentiate content tracks.

  • student track Fraunces + Bitter · Caramel, honey gold, vanilla · "Spicy Sundae"
  • designer track Outfit + Work Sans · Teal, menthol, cool white · "Menthol Steel"
  • nexus pages Fraunces + Work Sans · Forest green, black, white
  • apparatus Martian Mono + Zilla Slab · Concrete, rust/vermillion · "Formwork"

Each palette signals a distinct rhetorical register. Student pages are warm, embodied, material. Designer pages are cool, analytical, structural. Nexus pages synthesize with maximum contrast. Apparatus pages—this one included—expose the substrate. The monospace headers on these pages tell you that you've stepped outside the content and into the infrastructure that holds the content together.

On the Tie Sidebar

The fixed left sidebar displays a scanned necktie for each page: botanicals and florals for content tracks, geometrics and plaids for nexus pages. Neckties embody the project's central tension between constraint and expression. They are rare, decorative, and idiosyncratic—one of vanishingly few opportunities for individual ornament in the expressive desert of male professional fashion. A dev log entry on this site documents the selection and processing workflow.

build_stack

Tools and Infrastructure

The knowledge management system behind this webtext uses the same constraint-sequencing principles the course teaches. Notes accumulate in an Obsidian vault using a controlled vocabulary of wikilinks. Paired content and justification files create explicit dependency tracking between design decisions and their rationale.

  • knowledge mgmt Obsidian with Templater, custom CSS snippets
  • research db DEVONthink with full-text epub indexing
  • annotation Readwise → DEVONthink, Readwise → Obsidian
  • reading Apple Books, Yomu (alt)
  • sync Syncthing (local, cross-device)
  • version control Git with subtree split for selective public sync
  • static gen Quartz (pending configuration)

ai_disclosure

AI and LLM Use

This webtext was developed with substantial assistance from large language models, primarily Claude (Anthropic). The author used LLM conversation as a developmental editing and design iteration tool across multiple domains: conceptual development, structural planning, CSS prototyping, literature integration, and argument interrogation. All intellectual decisions, scholarly claims, and design choices are the author's; the LLM served as an interlocutor rather than a generator.

Specific uses included: testing argument structures against likely objections from composition studies audiences, generating CSS mockups for rapid visual iteration, organizing vault structures and file naming conventions, drafting placeholder content for structural testing, and identifying relevant scholarship for citation. The author reviewed, revised, and frequently rejected LLM suggestions. Conversation logs are available on request.

Why Disclose This Way

Composition studies asks students to document their writing processes. An article arguing for process visibility should practice what it documents. LLM use in scholarly production is neither shameful nor trivial; it is a writing technology, and this project treats writing as technology. The disclosure belongs here, in the apparatus, where infrastructure is examined rather than hidden.

accessibility

Accessibility

The webtext is designed for keyboard navigation with visible focus states. All images carry descriptive alt text. The four-palette color system maintains WCAG AA contrast ratios across all track combinations. Animations respect the prefers-reduced-motion media query. Body text is set at a minimum of 1.05rem with 1.7 line height. The blinking cursor element on apparatus pages degrades to a static indicator when motion preferences are set.

The fixed tie sidebar collapses to a horizontal banner on mobile viewports. Navigation remains accessible via the right-side panel, which stacks below content on narrow screens. The necktie images are decorative and non-essential; no information is conveyed solely through the tie patterns.
