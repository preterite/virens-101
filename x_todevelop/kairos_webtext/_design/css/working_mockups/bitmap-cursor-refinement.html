Observations

5×7 is my lean. At heading scale (1.3rem Fraunces), the individual pixels are visible without squinting — you can see the checkerboard construction — but the overall shape clearly reads as a rectangular cursor. The 5:7 aspect ratio happens to match classic terminal character cell proportions closely.

3×5 has the most dramatic grain but may tip toward "abstract pattern" rather than "cursor." The pixels are so large they compete with the letterforms.

5×9 and 7×11 are increasingly refined but lose the visible construction that makes this theoretically interesting. At 7×11, you essentially get a translucent green rectangle with a faint moire, which is pretty but misses the point.

40% opacity check: At all grid sizes, 0.4 reads as "present but subordinate" — the cursor doesn't compete with the heading text for attention, which is correct since its job is to contextualize, not dominate. The heading is the content; the cursor is the frame.

Corner framing: With odd grids, all four corners have filled pixels. This is most visible at 3×5 and 5×7; at higher resolutions the corners blend into the overall texture. The framing effect you wanted is strongest with fewer pixels.
