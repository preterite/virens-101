ECONOMIES OF COMPOSITION

Writing operates as technology. Capital substitutes for labor. The classroom becomes an economic system where constraints arrive in sequence and accumulation happens over time.

This article documents the design of VIRENS 101, a first-year composition course that integrates systems thinking with writing pedagogy. The innovation lies not in any single assignment but in the temporal architecture: when constraints arrive matters as much as what they are.

"Infrastructure is visible at breakdown."

— Star & Ruhleder

1.1 The Problem

Composition studies has inadequately integrated systems thinking. We treat economic forces as external pressures rather than recognizing economy already inside the classroom. Every constraint is a technology. Every technology performs an economic function.

This course brings economic theory to bear on composition pedagogy through constraint structure: the deliberate sequencing of writing constraints to enable textual accumulation. Students capture fragments, classify sources, route drafts through feedback systems, distill portfolios, connect ideas, and publish final arguments.

Design Principles

  • Constraint sequencing enables capital formation
  • Archives give you analytics for intervention
  • Difficulty operates as diagnostic of system state
  • Selection is composition at the synthesis stage

The six moves—capture, classify, route, distill, connect, publish—structure the semester. Each move introduces constraints that become infrastructure for subsequent moves. What begins as effort becomes stored capability.
