Writing operates as technology that substitutes capital for labor. This article documents the design of VIRENS 101, a first-year composition course integrating systems thinking with composition pedagogy through constraint structure.

The innovation lies in temporal architecture: when(constraints.arrive) matters as much as what(constraints.are). Students move through four movements across fifteen weeks, each introducing constraints that become infrastructure for subsequent work.

⚠ CORE INSIGHT

Economy already operates inside the classroom. Every constraint is technology. Every technology performs economic function. Traditional pedagogy obscures these relationships; constraint structure makes them visible.

02 : IMPLICATIONS_FOR_FIELD

This approach suggests several vectors for future work. Composition studies needs more robust integration of systems thinking, particularly concepts from infrastructure studies and design theory. The field would benefit from recovering process pedagogy against social turn critiques.

Most significantly, constraint structure provides alternative to genre theory's emphasis on uptake and situation. Rather than writers shaped by social forces, this framework positions writers as system designers who accumulate capital through deliberate constraint adoption.
