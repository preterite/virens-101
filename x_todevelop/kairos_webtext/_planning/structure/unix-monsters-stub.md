## Selected Unix Beasties (6+1)

From candidates including: tee, grep, sed, make, fork, chmod, kill, sudo, nohup

**Core 6 (ordered):**
1. `tee` — splits stream without consuming it; observation without interruption
2. `grep` — pattern extraction; constraint as generative
3. `make` — dependency tracking; incremental economy
4. `fork` — process duplication; autonomous copies that diverge
5. `chmod` — permission structure; designed access control
6. `sed` — stream editor; transformation in place

**+1 Special:**<br/>
7. `kill` — process termination; controlled ending

## Unix Beastie ↔ Monster Pairings

### 1. `tee` ↔ The Doppelgänger
- **`tee`**: Splits a stream into two identical outputs (simultaneous writing to file and stdout)
- **Doppelgänger**: The double occupying the same calendrical body (Columbus Day / Indigenous Peoples' Day)
- **Resonance**: Both embody **duplication without resolution** — two versions of the same thing existing simultaneously
- **Calendar moment**: Week 6–7 (Indigenous Peoples' Day)

### 2. `grep` ↔ The Changeling
- **`grep`**: Extracts patterns, finding what matches while filtering everything else
- **Changeling**: Recognized through **diagnostic pattern-matching** — "you've changed" is grep detecting difference
- **Resonance**: Both are about **recognition through search** — what you look for determines what you find
- **Calendar moment**: Week 13–14 (Thanksgiving)

### 3. `make` ↔ The Revenant
- **`make`**: Tracks dependencies and rebuilds only what's stale (knows what needs reanimation)
- **Revenant**: Returns **bearing marks of where it's been** — the Week 3 fragment revisited in Week 8
- **Resonance**: Both embody **selective resurrection** — not everything comes back, only what's needed
- **Calendar moment**: Week 7–8 (Homecoming)

### 4. `fork` ↔ The Golem
- **`fork`**: Creates an autonomous child process from a parent (duplication with divergence)
- **Golem**: **Animated by inscription** but denied full membership — serves but doesn't count
- **Resonance**: Both embody **derived labor** — the child process/golem performs work but in a separate, subordinate space
- **Calendar moment**: Week 10–11 (Veterans Day)

### 5. `chmod` ↔ The Mummy
- **`chmod`**: Controls permissions (read/write/execute) — **who can do what to the file**
- **Mummy**: **Wrapped in assessment packaging** — the reflective letter and grade as permission structure
- **Resonance**: Both are **preservation systems that prevent transformation** — chmod locks access; mummification arrests decomposition
- **Calendar moment**: Week 8–9 (Halloween)

### 6. `sed` ↔ The Specter
- **`sed`**: Stream editing — **transformation in place**, find-and-replace as continuous process
- **Specter**: **What persists after conditions have been destroyed** — Labor Day's extracted meaning
- **Resonance**: Both embody **invisible revision** — sed operates on streams as they pass; the specter conditions the present by absence
- **Calendar moment**: Week 1 (Labor Day)

### +1. `kill` ↔ The Vampire
- **`kill`**: Terminates runaway processes — **controlled ending of accumulation**
- **Vampire**: **Extracts surplus value** — drains what others produce
- **Resonance**: Both embody **intentional termination** — kill stops the process; vampire stops the circulation (by converting it to metrics)
- **Calendar moment**: No season (structural, not episodic)

## Pairing Logic

**Structural monsters** (vampire, specter, doppelgänger) pair with **structural operations** (kill, sed, tee)

**Recognition/transformation monsters** (changeling, revenant, golem) pair with **pattern/process operations** (grep, make, fork)

**Containment monsters** (mummy) pair with **access control operations** (chmod)

## Additional Context from Bestiary Development

### Monster Descriptions (from bestiary.md)

Each monster corresponds to:
- A moment in the academic calendar
- A stage in the corpse → compost → garden arc
- Appears without announcement in Grant's or Vera's narrative scenes
- The resonances are dysraphic — seams visible, joints don't close

**The Vampire** (no season): Surplus-value extraction. Drains what others produce. Institutional consumption of student writing labor converted to metrics without returning value. Two puncture wounds: grade on transcript, checked box on outcomes matrix.

**The Specter** (Week 1 / Labor Day): What persists after conditions have been destroyed or forgotten. Neither present nor absent. Labor Day as ghost of a labor holiday — meaning extracted, leaving a day off whose referent has become spectral.

**The Doppelgänger** (Week 6-7 / Indigenous Peoples' Day): Two holidays occupy the same calendrical body. Columbus Day written over by Indigenous Peoples' Day, neither fully erasing the other. The palimpsest visible: this day means two things that cannot be reconciled.

**The Revenant** (Week 7-8 / Homecoming): The past returns with a body. Alumni walking among present students. The fragment that returns is recognizably the same and recognizably not — it has been through decomposition, reclassification, contact with other texts.

**The Mummy** (Week 8-9 / Halloween): The cheapest costume. Toilet paper and a living body. Actual mummification preserves for an afterlife not yet arrived at. The portfolio sealed by reflective letter — textual corpus embalmed against decomposition, wrapped in assessment packaging.

**The Golem** (Week 10-11 / Veterans Day): Animated by inscription. _Emet_ (truth) on forehead = life; erase one letter → _met_ (death). Serves faithfully, protects community, denied full membership. Grant's Filipino-American GI-descended family — veterans animated by inscription, status rescinded by Rescission Act of 1946.

**The Changeling** (Week 13-14 / Thanksgiving): The child who comes back wrong. Three months of decomposition/reclassification, then return home. "You've changed" is the folk diagnosis. The dissonance is the changeling's uncanny presence at the family table.
