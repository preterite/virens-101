# Fragment: Circulation as Site of Value

## Core

Trimbur's [[@trimbur2000compp]] recovery of delivery/circulation as neglected canon connects to Horner's [[@horner2000terms]] materialist emphasis: value isn't just produced, it _moves_. The "product" is partly the pattern of movement, not just the final artifact.

## Elaboration

The course builds a circulation system—fragments move through accumulation, through peer exchange, through reflection, through revision. This framework positions composition as _producing_ value through organized circulation—not derivative but generative. The value of a fragment isn't fixed at production; it changes as the fragment moves through different contexts and uses. Marx would say no, machines don't produce value; corollary, systems (technologies) don't produce value. However, what the emphasis on circulation does is foreground _distribution_ and _reproduction_ as sites of potential value appropriation by diverse parties, in addition to what composition has always foregrounded: production and consumption as the sites where value gets appropriated.
