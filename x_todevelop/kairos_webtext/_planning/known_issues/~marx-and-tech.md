# Fragment: Marx and Technology

A more precisely Marxist revision of the reductive and compacted axiom that "technology substitutes capital for labor" [[600_teaching/virens_101/x_todevelop/kairos_webtext/content/nexus/_acer|_acer]] would argue that capitalists use technological change to substitute machinery for 'living labor' in production, reducing necessary labor time, displacing workers, and thereby increasing relative surplus value.

In _Capital_ vol. 1 [[@?Marx1867Capi]] Marx argues that capitalists replace living labor with machinery ("dead labor") in the labor process. Capitalist development tends toward a rising ratio of constant capital to variable capital. Capitalists seek to increase relative surplus value by cheapening labor-power. However, for Marx, machinery can only transfer value: only living labor can produce new value.

In the _Grundrisse_ [@?Marx1892Gru]] Marx suggests a shift wherein the machinery of fixed capital becomes an "automatic system," and the worker's role is reduced toward maintenance. Living labor becomes a moment within a system dominated by objectified labor realized as machinery or technique. For Marx, the productive power appears as power of capital embodied in machinery, and living labor is pushed out of direct production. However, Marx frames that circumstance as a contradiction, in that the capitalist attempts to reduce necessary labor time through the use of machinery while value still depends on labor time.
