## Theoretical Breakthroughs / Key Developments

### Interactive Fiction Echo as Triple-Coded Second-Person Voice

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): arboretum, webtext-architecture, ergodic-navigation
- Type: architectural, conceptual
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: this digest

**Discovery:** The arboretum page's second-person address ("You are standing...") simultaneously performs three registers: (1) field-naturalist guide voice, (2) interactive fiction parser prompt (Zork/Adventure), and (3) pedagogical direct address. The opening paraphrases Zork's "You are standing in an open field west of a white house" (1980); the closing paraphrases Crowther and Woods's Adventure: "You are standing at the end of a road before a small brick building. Around you is a forest. A small stream flows out of the building and down a gully" (1976). The chronology is inverted — derivative first, progenitor last — enacting the webtext's argument about tracing dependency chains backward to sources.

**Implications:**
- The arboretum page performs ergodic, choice-driven navigation without ever naming it — form IS argument, avoiding assembly-manual problem
- The attenuated nav (Explore / Switch Track only) reads as parser game's limited verb set — no GO BACK
- The closing frame at the ravine bottom places the reader literally inside the pot/cave, looking up at air and light — van Helmont's answer
- Paraphrases can outlink to IF references (playable Zork, Adventure source, "Get Lamp" documentary) without explanation
- Only readers who recognize the echoes will catch the IF layer; others get a walk through trees — this IS the experience-good argument

**Next steps:** Draft closing frame with Adventure echo flowing into van Helmont passage. Locate Powers *Overstory* "bucket experiment" passage for possible reference.

### Arboretum as Descending Terrain with Microclimate Logic

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): arboretum, webtext-architecture
- Type: architectural
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: this digest

**Discovery:** The 12 trees organized into 4 landscape groups where topography creates microclimates that justify botanically impossible adjacencies. The walk descends continuously — from open field to ravine bottom — and the terrain shift carries experiential and argumentative weight.

**The four groups and their landscapes:**

1. **Oak–Elm–Maple (The Commons):** Campus-like open lawn, broad canopy, gravel path. Familiar, legible landscape. One massive white oak; elms possibly including one stressed/dead (DED ghost); maple where ground dips, near a low stone wall. The walker is comfortable.

2. **Oleander–Yew–Holly (The Toxic Garden):** Path narrows into a south-facing walled section creating a microclimate pocket. All three species are deadly (unannounced). Oleander is a mass planting — proliferated, lush, showy. Only 2–3 yews — ancient, dark, still, possibly hollow-trunked. Holly in the understory, dark, spiny, berries as only bright point. Enclosed, shadowed, slightly ominous. Moss and leaf litter.

3. **Cedar–Alder–Hemlock (The Wet Forest):** Path opens onto water. Cedar planted as hedge-like screen/threshold. Alder along stream bank, pale mottled bark. Hemlock overhead closing the canopy — deep green filtered light, needle duff, silence. Boardwalk over wet ground. The walker is INSIDE a system, not viewing specimens. Most immersive section.

4. **Star Anise–Olive–Tupelo (The Ravine):** Boardwalk ends, ground tilts, stream audible below. Topography creates microclimates solving the impossible-combination problem: olive at dry sun-exposed rim, star anise on sheltered moist slope (fragrance hits before sight), tupelo at saturated bottom with standing water. Walk descends. Closing frame happens at the bottom.

**Experiential arc:** familiar → enclosed/dangerous → immersive/systemic → descended/transformed

**Path surface shifts:** gravel → flagstone → boardwalk → bare earth/stone descent (register change the body feels)

## Architectural Decisions / Structural Changes

### Closing Frame at Ravine Bottom

**Decision:** The arboretum walk ends at the bottom of the ravine, not with an ascent or return. The closing frame (van Helmont's bucket experiment) happens at the lowest point.

**Rationale:** Thematically perfect — the reader is in the pot, looking up at where the mass came from (air and light). The insight is humble, not panoramic. Van Helmont got his answer from a pot on the ground. The Adventure echo ("end of a road... a small stream flows out... and down a gully") lands naturally at a low point with water. The walk's one-way descent matches the constraint-structure argument: the sequence is not reversible.

**Implications for:**
- Nav: Attenuated nav at bottom reads as parser's limited verb set — Explore / Switch Track only, no "return to top"
- Reader experience: No panoramic vista, no summary; the end is immersive, not reflective
- IF echo: Inverted chronology (Zork → Adventure) parallels descent from derivative to progenitor

### Species Binomials for Group 1

**Decision:** Quercus alba (white oak), Ulmus americana (American elm), Acer saccharum (sugar maple)

**Rationale:**
- Q. alba: The iconic open-grown American oak with massive horizontal branching — right for the lone lawn specimen
- U. americana: Requires the DED story (the tree whose absence shaped streetscapes)
- A. saccharum: Sugar maple over red maple (A. rubrum) because the sap-tapping economy is the whole point — invisible infrastructure made visible

**Implications for:**
- Nexus pages: Need to decide whether nexus pages use genus-only names (quercus.html) or full binomials (quercus-alba.html). Current convention appears genus-only. Recommend keeping genus-only for nexus, reserving binomials for arboretum body text.

### IF Echoes Are Enacted, Never Named

**Decision:** No sentence in the arboretum text will ever explain the interactive fiction framing. The form IS the argument. Naming it would be the assembly-manual problem the documentation fiction is designed to avoid.

**Rationale:** The reader who catches zero IF echoes still gets a walk through trees. The reader who catches all three registers (naturalist / parser / pedagogical) understands the page is performing the ergodic navigation structure the webtext embodies. This enacts the experience-good argument at the page level.
