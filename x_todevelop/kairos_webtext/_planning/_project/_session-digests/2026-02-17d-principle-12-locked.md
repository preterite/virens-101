## Architectural Decisions / Structural Changes

### Decomposition Is Constraint Sequencing

**Decision:** #12's elaboration now defines decomposition as the separation of textual operations from the experiential labor that produced their configuration — connecting it directly to #8 (constraint arrival). Decomposition frees operations from a particular constraint sequence so they can be reinvested across future sequences.

**Source of insight:** ENV passage on experience goods: "The course distinguishes between operations that can be decomposed and delegated (routing, classification, formatting, templating) and labor whose value is constituted in the temporal encounter itself." Decomposition separates the delegable operations from the experiential encounter that configured them.

**Implications for:**
- #8 / #12 relationship: The cycle now closes through constraint sequencing logic rather than vague return-to-beginning
- Nyssa tree narrative: may need review to ensure it reflects the constraint-sequencing connection rather than purely ecological framing
- Nexus page development: the decomposition-as-constraint-sequencing claim is a strong candidate for elaboration

### Fixed/Liquid Capital Replaces Entropy

**Decision:** Thermodynamic vocabulary (low-entropy, high-entropy) replaced with economic vocabulary (fixed capital, liquid capital). "Publication preserves completed texts into fixed capital" opens the elaboration; "recirculate as liquid capital" describes what decomposition produces.

**Rationale:** No other principle imports physics vocabulary. The economic register is primary throughout the twelve principles; secondary metaphors (ecological, technological) serve the economic argument rather than competing with it.

### Ecological Metaphor Subordinated to Economic Argument

**Decision:** The sentence "The metaphor is ecological:" was cut. The ecological image ("A dead organism that remains intact persists but does not nourish; one that decomposes feeds new growth") remains but arrives without metacritical announcement, consistent with how other elaborations handle secondary frameworks.

**Rationale:** Michael's observation that ecology is a "greedy metaphor" because it operates across both space and time — it can silently absorb the temporal argument into a geographic one. The economic framework's advantage is foregrounding time as the operative dimension. The corpse-compost-garden sequence is a chronology, not a landscape.

### Pedagogical Register Diagnosis Corrected

**Decision:** The prior digest flagged "pedagogical register in claim block" as an issue. Review determined this was a misdiagnosis — the "Pedagogies that emphasize..." sentences appear in the elaboration, consistent with other locked principles (#3, #8). Two issues, not three.

## Process Note: "Not X But Y" Formulation

Michael flagged a persistent rhetorical pattern — "not X but Y" — as a synchronic replacement move that conflicts with the webtext's temporal, associational argument. The formulation freezes two things side by side and picks a winner, which is the logic the argument is trying to get past. The original #12 elaboration contained "Decomposition is not destruction but transformation" — a textbook instance. The revision defines decomposition positively through what it does (separates operations from experiential labor, recirculates as liquid capital) rather than negatively against what it isn't. This observation applies beyond #12 and is worth watching in future drafting.
