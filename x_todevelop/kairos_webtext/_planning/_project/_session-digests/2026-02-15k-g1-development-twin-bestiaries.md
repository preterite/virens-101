## Theoretical Breakthroughs / Key Developments

### Option 1 Belongs in G2, Not G1

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): fiction-grant, scene-g1, scene-g2
- Type: structural
- Dashboard-worthy: no
- Captured in: scene-outline-g1.md Draft Notes

**Discovery:** The misrouted index card (Option 1) does corpse/compost theoretical work that requires the reader to have enough framework to register the card's journey between two infrastructures. In G1, Grant hasn't produced enough course material for the card to carry conceptual charge. In G2 (Weeks 7-8), Grant is actively working in both library and Eli Review systems simultaneously — the card traveling between systems would physically trigger the "I do this all day" recognition rather than requiring it to arrive through abstract reflection. The card makes the parallel concrete at the moment when it has stakes.

### TuTh Schedule for Dramatic Reasons

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): fiction-grant, fiction-vera, all scenes
- Type: architectural
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: all scene outlines, character doc

**Discovery:** TuTh serves the narrative better than MWF across multiple dimensions. Labor Day (always Monday) doesn't disrupt the class schedule, keeping the holiday story entirely in the library domain. The 75-minute session (3 blocks) gives Vera enough instructional time that Grant's paralysis reads as genuine overwhelm rather than laziness. The longer gap between meetings (TuTh rhythm vs. MWF's every-other-day) makes homework pile up unprocessed and makes the library feel like Grant's primary world — the course as twice-weekly intrusion rather than constant presence. Additionally, the dr_green_virens voice model document already has Vera teaching on Tuesday.

### Twin Bestiaries: Domesticating the Alien / Alienating the Domestic

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): bestiary, nexus-nyssa, meta-structure
- Type: conceptual
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: frag-24-twin-bestiaries.md

**Discovery:** A Technological Bestiary (Unix/infrastructure metaphors) and a Teratological Bestiary (monster/creature metaphors) could share a single two-column nexus page. One domesticates the alien; the other alienates the domestic. Together they perform denaturalization in opposite directions. Frankenstein is the dual-nature entry appearing in both columns — both technological (assembled, animated by technique) and teratological (monster, shouldn't be alive). The medieval bestiary's pre-Linnaean epistemology (pelican and basilisk in the same book) imports the stance that the distinction between "real" technical terminology and "figurative" monster language is itself a naturalized boundary the article wants to disturb. Unix terms like "zombie process" and "orphan process" are already teratological, further dissolving the two-column boundary from within.

### Multiples of Six as Emergent Structural Pattern

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): meta-structure
- Type: structural
- Dashboard-worthy: no
- Captured in: frag-25-multiples-of-six.md

**Discovery:** Structural units keep falling into multiples of 6 (12 trees, 6+6 scenes, 24 fragments, 6 moves, 6 blocks/week). These are timekeeping numbers (hours, months, day-hours) for a project theorizing temporality. Design posture: not "build to a grid" but "notice the grid and don't break it without reason." Frag-25 itself is the 25th fragment — the productive remainder that broke the pattern by describing it.

### Modern Library Circ Desk as Scene Infrastructure

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): fiction-grant, scene-g1
- Type: evidentiary
- Dashboard-worthy: no
- Captured in: scene-outline-g1.md Draft Notes

**Discovery:** RFID pads (no magnetic strips), ILS transaction logging under staff login (latent surveillance of Grant's labor — every return timestamped and attributed without anyone checking), scan-check-route flag decisions (hold/ILL/reserve/clean → different destinations = classification-and-routing), equipment checkouts (shows Grant interacting with studying peers while working), no self-checkout kiosk (all patron interaction through Grant). The ILS transaction log is the thematic goldmine: the surveillance infrastructure that captures Grant's labor already exists and is already counting him, but passively. This exactly parallels what Grant will later fear the six-moves framework could become. Not narrated explicitly in G1; the ILS screen simply visible on the desk.
