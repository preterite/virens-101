## Theoretical Breakthroughs / Key Developments

### #5 Consequence-First Revision

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): principle-5, experience-good, constraint-sequencing
- Type: structural
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: trees_principles.md

**Discovery/Development:** The old #5 claim led with the category label ("Generation is an experience good: [properties]"). The revision leads with the design consequence: "The labor of generation must, as a matter of economic necessity, be protected from evaluative constraint." The experience-good term arrives last in the elaboration, naming what the reader has already understood.

**Key analytical moves:**
- What "experience good" reveals that Elbow's vocabulary doesn't: makes destruction structural rather than psychological; provides design principle for constraint sequencing; explains why generation can't be previewed, outsourced, or shortcutted
- Two differends avoided: "not as indulgence but as" (creates opposition it must resolve) and "economic claim, not psychological" (say what it does, not what it is)
- "Structurally destroyed" does economic work without announcing the distinction
- Tricolon from old version ("realized through the doing, assessed only retrospectively, destroyed by premature interruption") eliminated as redundant — all three properties already shown through felt-sense example and foreclosed-value language

### Machine Delegation Is Not a Standalone Principle

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): principle-9, machine-delegation, tree-alignment
- Type: architectural
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: trees_principles.md

**Discovery/Development:** Systematic inventory of course materials vs. webtext materials showed citation-as-dependency has overwhelming structural support (79 course files, 23 webtext files, dedicated reasons file, fully drafted nexus page, 12/15 weekly overviews, BEAM framework assignment) while machine delegation is a specific application concentrated in 3-4 documents (AI spec test, AI integrity policy, one weekly overview). Machine delegation's content already lives naturally in the Tsuga nexus page ("the formatting is delegable to machines... the course frees class time for the dependency analysis that machines cannot perform").

**Implications:**
- Machine delegation falls out of the intersection of #5 (experience good — some labor can't be delegated), #3 (technology substitutes capital for labor — some labor should be delegated), and #8 (constraint sequencing — the question is when and which, not whether)
- Citation-as-dependency is the principle that earns a seat at the table of twelve
- The claim block sequence was misaligned with tree positions from #9 onward; realignment resolved this

## Architectural Decisions / Structural Changes

### Claim Block #9–11 Realignment

**Decision:** Move Citation as Dependency from #10 to #9 (Tsuga position). Move Reading as Productive Consumption from #11 to #10. Create Circulation as new #11 (Illicium position). Remove Machine Delegation as standalone principle.

**Rationale:** The twelve tree positions (spelling QUANTITATION) and the twelve claim/elaboration blocks must map 1:1. Machine Delegation was occupying Tsuga's slot (#9), which properly belongs to Citation as Dependency. The displacement cascaded: citation-as-dependency displaced circulation, and the blocks from #9 onward didn't match their tree positions.

**Alternatives considered:** Keeping machine delegation and finding it a different tree position — rejected because the inventory showed it lacks the structural depth of a standalone principle.

**Implications for:**
- Work sequence: Circulation (#11) is now the sole unwritten principle and the next generative task
- Content organization: Machine delegation content needs redistribution plan (deferred)
- Tree alignment: All 12 positions now have correct concept assignments

### Reading as Productive Consumption Self-Citation Remedy

**Decision:** Remove "Edwards, in progress manuscript" citation from #10 claim. "Activating labor" now stands as the principle's own term.

**Rationale:** Self-citation of unpublished work in the claim created a dependency on an unavailable source for the principle's central term. The term does its own work without external authorization.
