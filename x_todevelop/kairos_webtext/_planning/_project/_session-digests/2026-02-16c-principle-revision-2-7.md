## Theoretical Breakthroughs / Key Developments

### Naming → Specifying as Distinct Steps

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): operational-vocabulary
- Type: conceptual
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: principle 2 elaboration (final version)

**Discovery:** Naming and specifying are distinct operations: naming creates the addressable entity; specifying attaches attributes to that entity. Without "capture" as a name, you cannot say "capture comes before distillation" — not because the relationship doesn't exist, but because you have no way to pick it out. The filename/attributes analogy: you need a filename before you can assign attributes, but many people stop at naming = specifying.

**Implications:**
- The elaboration needed to make the dependency explicit: "Once named, operations can carry further specifications (proportion, relation) that enable deliberate sequencing beyond temporal succession."
- "Specifiable" replaces "countable" as the hinge word — broader, more accurate, avoids the narrow "literally counting instances" reading
- The naming→specifying→sequencing chain is the real logic the old "countable, and therefore designable" was compressing

### Writer Agency as Principle 7's Center of Gravity

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): permission-flags, constraint-sequencing
- Type: architectural
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: principle 7 claim and elaboration (final version)

**Discovery:** Original principle 7 centered on pedagogical design (teacher redistributes constraints). Colleague feedback revealed the real claim is about writer agency (writer learns to analyze and manage constraints). These are two different claims at two different scales: teacher sequences constraint *arrival* (#8); writer develops constraint *literacy* (#7). Splitting the labor between #7 and #8 this way is cleaner than having both principles compete for the design perspective.

**Implications:**
- Principle 7 now foregrounds the writer/student as agent of analysis, not the teacher as omniscient sequencer
- The four properties (visibility, rationale, alterability, ownership) arrive in the elaboration as analytical tools rather than in the claim as taxonomy
- "Alterability" replaces "writability" at claim level — implies writer's potential action on the constraint; "writability" with its UNIX resonance reserved for the nexus page where permission flags framework gets full development

## Architectural Decisions / Structural Changes

### Principle 2: Six Moves List Deferred from Claim to Elaboration

**Decision:** The six named moves (capture, classify, route, distill, connect, publish) no longer appear in the claim sentence. They appear in the elaboration with natural-language glosses: "notice (capture), sort (classify), direct (route), compress (distill), link (connect), release into circulation (publish)."

**Rationale:** Colleague feedback identified "computational overlay" problem — listing the moves in the claim front-loaded systems-engineering vocabulary before establishing why a comp reader should care. Leading with consequence (operations become distinguishable, specifiable, sequenceable) lets the reader understand the payoff before encountering the vocabulary.

**Implications for:**
- The nexus page can still introduce the moves prominently, but the abstract-level claim now leads with what the vocabulary enables rather than what it contains
- "Notice" replacing "take in material" for capture's gloss softens the computational register and implies attentional agency

### Principle 7: "In Addition To" Framing for #2

**Decision:** Principle 2's claim reads "in addition to the temporal stages writers move through" rather than "rather than" or "instead of."

**Rationale:** Defuses the "are you replacing process pedagogy?" objection before it forms. The colleague's feedback was partly gesturing toward this concern. "In addition to" positions the operational vocabulary as complementary lens, consistent with the elaboration in trees_principles.md.

### Principle 7: Closing Sentence Reorientation

**Decision:** Final sentence of #7 elaboration: "Composing contexts may impose demands upon writers for increased analytical capacity over decreased rhetorical constraint."

**Rationale:** Multiple iterations tested different capstone sentences. This version avoids the "not by escaping constraints but by..." formulation (which was strong but belonged more to a claim than an elaboration closer) and instead frames the insight as a general principle about what composing actually requires. It counters the assumption that agency = fewer constraints by asserting that composing contexts demand *more* analytical capacity, not *less* constraint.

## Discarded Directions

- **Leading with the four-property list in the #7 claim:** Rejected because it front-loaded analytical apparatus before establishing stakes. Colleague's feedback was specifically about opacity of the property list without context. Properties now appear in elaboration where the reader is primed.
- **"Countable" as the hinge word for #2:** Replaced by "specifiable." "Countable" was too narrow (sounded like literally counting instances) and too compressed (elided the sequencing step). "Specifiable" captures the full chain without the ambiguity.
- **Including concrete sequencing examples in #2 elaboration:** Cut for compression. Examples ("foreground capture before distillation, defer connection until material accumulates") were strong demonstrations but the elaboration defers to the nexus page for concrete illustration. The abstract-level elaboration stays with principle.
- **Reversing the triage order in #7's three-part negotiation sequence:** Considered putting "claiming ownership" first for rhetorical uplift. Kept current order (recognizing immovable → understanding rationale → claiming ownership) because it reads as a growth progression from constraint acceptance to constraint agency — which is the developmental trajectory the principle describes.
