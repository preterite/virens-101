## Theoretical Breakthroughs / Key Developments

### Principle #1 as Visualization Claim, Not Classification Claim

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): writing-as-economic-cycle
- Type: conceptual
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: this digest (claim formulation below)

**Discovery:** The original #1 claim ("not metaphor but actual economic cycle") was a classification move asking readers to assent to an ontological category. The revised claim is a visualization move: attending to information movement and value appropriation *reveals* conditions that *then become available* for intervention. This reframes the foundational wager from "agree that writing is economic" to "here's what you can see and do when you track economic relationships in writing."

**Implications:**
- #1 earns its first position honestly: you have to see before you can name (#2), accumulate (#3), sequence (#8), or redesign anything
- The "not metaphor" framing is vestigial and reintroduces a differend between economy and meaning that the claim shouldn't create — "not metaphor" reads as "not meaning," shutting down free play when the actual argument is that managing information flows *clears space* for meaning-making
- "Appropriation" belongs in #1 from the start as the foundational wager, announcing the stakes even though it becomes a full nexus node at position #11
- Writer remains the agent of visualization throughout: "when writers attend... they reveal... and thereby facilitate"

### Economy/Meaning Differend as Design Problem

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): writing-as-economic-cycle
- Type: conceptual
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: this digest

**Discovery:** The problem with "economic" as a predicate is that it defers or hides "value" the same way "metaphor" defers or hides "meaning." The framework that makes economy and meaning mutually exclusive is the real obstacle. The intervention isn't "economy not meaning" but rather: "managing efficiencies in terms of where information goes can clear out space to think about meaning where the writer gets to decide who appropriates value." Economy serves the humanistic project rather than displacing it. This can't be solved in a sentence, but the claim can avoid *creating* the differend it would then have to resolve.

**Implications:**
- "Not metaphor" language removed entirely from claim, needs removal from elaboration too
- "Economic" functions as method (tracking the economic dimension reveals...) not as predicate (writing *is* economic)
- The elaboration must establish the economy/meaning relationship without declaring a winner

### Four-Tier Diagnostic of Remaining 10 Principles

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): cross-cutting (all twelve principles)
- Type: architectural
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: this digest

**Discovery:** Systematic review of all 10 non-revised principles against the diagnostic that identified #2 and #7's problems (foregrounding apparatus over operational consequences). Produced a four-tier ranking:

- **Tier 1 (closest to old #2/#7 problem):** #9 Machine Delegation (taxonomy-forward, "irreducibly experiential" is premise not conclusion, consequence is generic), #5 Generation as Experience Good (definition-forward, comp readers ask "why do I need this term for what Elbow already told me," consequence only negative)
- **Tier 2 (structural issues):** #11 Reading as Productive Consumption (self-citation of unpublished work in claim sentence, "activating labor" bootstraps its own authority), #1 Writing as Economic Cycle (negative definition, vague "real-world value," consequence buried in elaboration)
- **Tier 3 (localized):** #10 Citation as Dependency (double-barreled, second half vague), #12 Decomposition Over Preservation (mixed registers — economic argument + ecological metaphor + thermodynamic vocabulary)
- **Tier 4 (working well):** #3 Textual Accumulation, #4 Generation Before Evaluation, #6 Difficulty as Diagnostic, #8 Constraint Arrival

**Implications:**
- #8 (Constraint Arrival) identified as model claim: provocative, testable, apparatus-free — "the temporal sequence in which constraints arrive shapes learning outcomes more decisively than the content of those constraints"
- Pattern across weak claims: they front-load *categories* rather than *consequences*
- The fix that worked for #2 and #7 (lead with what the principle enables, defer framework vocabulary to elaboration) applies to #9 and #5 most directly

## Formulation Candidates / Key Phrasings

1. **[writing-as-economic-cycle]** "When writers attend to the movement of information and the appropriation of textual value, they reveal the conditions and processes under which writing's labor transforms into capital, and thereby facilitate the analysis, sequencing, and revision of those conditions and processes." — Context: near-final principle #1 claim. Visualization structure: attend → reveal → facilitate. Writer as agent throughout. "Appropriation" announces the wager.

2. **[writing-as-economic-cycle]** "Technologies substitute capital for labor. Writing is a technology. The substitutions that occur in processes of writing are economic processes with stages that can be acted upon." — Context: opening of revised #1 elaboration. Short declarative syllogism replacing the old conditional. Sentence 3 flagged as problematic compression — may be redundant if sentences 2 and 4 connect directly.

3. **[writing-as-economic-cycle]** "When writers attend to this substitution, they can map a cycle of production, distribution, use, and reproduction that describes what happens as texts move through composing, sharing, reading, and remaking." — Context: elaboration sentence connecting technology definition to cycle mapping. Double mapping (economic stages → composing stages) earns the economic vocabulary.

4. **[writing-as-economic-cycle]** "The tools and vocabulary of economic analysis — value, labor, capital, appropriation — become applicable in the act of writing." — Context: elaboration sentence. "In the act of writing" locates economic vocabulary inside composing itself rather than in retrospective scholarly analysis. Note tension: this narrows scope from "composition classroom" (institutional) to "act of writing" (practice). Both defensible; institutional version more provocative for comp audience.

5. **[cross-cutting]** "#8 is the model claim: provocative, testable, apparatus-free." — Context: diagnostic benchmark for evaluating all other claims against.
