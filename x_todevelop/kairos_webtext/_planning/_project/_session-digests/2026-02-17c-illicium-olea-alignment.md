## Architectural Decisions / Structural Changes

### Illicium Introduces the Durability Premise That #11 Builds On

**Decision:** Illicium (position 10) now establishes that writing's inscriptive durability is what makes circulation *traceable* rather than merely present. This is the premise #11's claim block deploys ("Motion requires a durable artifact to trace and act upon") without repeating the establishment.

**Rationale:** Position 10 precedes the #11 claim block in the QUANTITATION sequence. The tree narrative is the natural site for introducing the premise that the claim then assumes. This follows the pattern where tree narratives do ground-clearing and source-attribution work, while claim blocks state principles without attribution.

**Implications for:**
- Illicium nexus page: can elaborate the Babbage/Barlow synthesis (mill/store, information in motion) that the tree narrative compresses
- Reading sequence: a reader encountering Illicium before #11 gets the durability premise; a reader entering at #11 gets it compressed in the elaboration — both paths work

### Olea Receives From #11 Rather Than Re-Establishing

**Decision:** Olea's opening now assumes the diversity of appropriation (#11's contribution) and extends to the analytical apparatus for working within that diversity. The old opening did conceptual work #11 now handles.

**Rationale:** Follows the architectural principle established in the #11 drafting session: claim blocks do the conceptual work, tree narratives do source attribution and scene-level enactment.

### Two Complementary Taxonomies Coordinated

**Decision:** #11's claim block names *forms* of appropriation (self-directed, gift, communal, institutional, market). Olea names *participants and positions* (students, instructors, institutions, peers, discourse community). These are now explicitly presented as complementary lenses rather than uncoordinated lists.

**Rationale:** The old Olea listed participants while #11 listed forms, creating apparent inconsistency. Making the distinction explicit (forms vs. positions) turns a coordination problem into an analytical feature: you can ask both "what form does this appropriation take?" and "who occupies which positions within it?"

### Gibson-Graham / Resnick-Wolff Division of Labor Clarified

**Decision:** Gibson-Graham provides the *map* (diversity of appropriation forms). Resnick and Wolff provide the *analytical tool* (who labors, who receives surplus, what sustains the arrangement). Gibson-Graham is now explicitly attributed in Olea (appropriate since #11's claim block follows the no-attribution pattern). Resnick/Wolff is positioned as the finer-grained framework for analyzing internal structure within any single form.

**Rationale:** The old text named both as "particularly relevant" without differentiating. The new formulation gives each source a distinct analytical role and gives Olea a theoretical contribution distinct from #11.
