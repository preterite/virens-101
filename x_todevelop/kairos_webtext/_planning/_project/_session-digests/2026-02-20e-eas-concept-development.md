## Theoretical Breakthroughs / Key Developments

### 1. The Access Model Diagnosis

**Metadata:**
- Thread(s): eas, disciplinary-history
- Type: conceptual
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: eas-integrated-draft.md

**Discovery:** Expressivism and social constructionism, despite appearing opposed, share a structural premise: both are *access models* that presume the pedagogy's relationship to interiority is one of reaching it. Expressivism assumes access to a prior self; the pedagogical uptake of social constructionism assumes access to constituting forces. The EAS identifies this shared premise as the thing to replace.

**Implications:**
- Berlin's taxonomy (1988) *needed* expressivism to be a naive access model so social-epistemic rhetoric could supersede it — a problem of disciplinary reception, not necessarily of the underlying theories
- The social turn's genuine achievement (making visible constitutive forces) carried a hidden cost: making the particular learner theoretically suspect
- The EAS is positioned not as rejecting either tradition but as removing the access *requirement* both share

**Evidence:** Berlin 1988, Faigley 1986, Bartholomae-Elbow 1995 exchange. Crowley (1998) on institutional formations. Gradin (1995) as revisionary account of expressivism.

### 2. Necessarily Variable Access as Key Differentiator

**Metadata:**
- Thread(s): eas
- Type: conceptual
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: eas-integrated-draft.md

**Discovery:** The EAS's distinctive claim is not "we can't know" (skepticism) but that access to interiority is *necessarily variable*: it varies across individuals, within a single individual over time, and across modalities. This variability is not a solvable problem but a permanent condition of embodied cognition — "nonnegotiable variance." This epistemologizes Bridle's *New Dark Age* condition for the pedagogical context.

**Implications:**
- Distinguishes EAS from common-sense "students are mysterious"
- Creates the design mandate: build for the failure case (defensive design)
- Explains why access-assuming pedagogies "fail silently" for students whose access profiles don't match assumptions
- The teacher can't diagnose the mismatch because interiority is precisely what's inaccessible

### 3. Accountability Without Transparency

**Metadata:**
- Thread(s): eas, assessment, constraint-sequencing
- Type: conceptual + methodological
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: eas-integrated-draft.md (assessment section and Butler-Glissant development)

**Discovery:** The Butler-Glissant synthesis produces a principle stronger than either alone: opacity is both *unavoidable* (Butler: formation is not fully recoverable in narrative) and *nonnegotiable* (Glissant: refusing comprehension is an ethical entitlement). The operational translation is "accountability without transparency": writers can be held accountable for observable textual work without being required to disclose or perform interiority for institutional assessment.

**Implications:**
- Course outcomes attach to observable textual work, not graded sincerity or narratable self-knowledge
- The accumulation model makes this tractable: a semester's corpus is evidence about what a writer can do under constraint
- Connects to Inoue complement: Inoue redesigns assessment around labor-time; EAS redesigns around textual accumulation. Different inputs, potentially compatible outputs
- Assessment consequences should be developed further in the article's pedagogical sections

### 4. The Elbow Recovery Sharpened

**Metadata:**
- Thread(s): elbow-recovery, eas
- Type: conceptual
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: eas-integrated-draft.md (final two paragraphs)

**Discovery:** The EAS reframes Elbow as production-for-surprise rather than excavation. Freewriting is valuable not because it accesses a buried self but because it produces material that surprises the writer. The EAS removes the access *requirement* while preserving the access *possibility*. Late Elbow (*Vernacular Eloquence*, "The Believing Game") was already closer to this position than Berlin's taxonomy permitted the field to see. Constraint sequencing reveals the social mechanism always implicit in Elbovian practice: generation precedes evaluation because it creates material for peer response, revision, and accumulation to operate on.

**Implications:**
- The expressivist and the social were never as far apart as the taxonomies suggested — this is the article's closing claim for this section
- Gradin (1995) provides scholarly precedent for the recovery
- "The believing game produces texts, not truths about the self" — key formulation

## Architectural Decisions / Structural Changes

### EAS Section Structure: 9-Step Arc Established

**Decision:** The EAS argument follows a 9-step progression, organized into three clusters: (1) the disciplinary problem (access model → Berlin mechanism → cost), (2) the alternative and its grounds (EAS definition → ethical warrants → epistemological warrants), (3) design consequences (defensive design → pedagogical architecture → assessment → Elbow recovery).

**Rationale:** The self-critique in marx_and_eas.md identified the need for an explicit causal chain from EAS premise to constraints/accumulation. The 9-step structure makes each inferential step visible and separable for distribution across nexus pages or webtext sections.

**Alternatives considered:** The original 500-800 word definition felt too compressed to carry the genealogical and grounding work. The full 9-step arc can be split across fragments for the webtext while remaining traceable as a single argument.

**Implications for:**
- Work sequence: EAS draft is ready for fragment integration; needs placement in article/webtext architecture
- Reading: Thomas Kent / post-process needs engagement (sentence or footnote level)
- Content organization: The three clusters (problem / alternative / design) are natural division points for webtext distribution

### Presentation Strategy: Design Principle, Not Third Way

**Decision:** Present the EAS as "a disciplinary design principle that reorganizes pedagogy and assessment under acknowledged opacity," not as "a third way that finally escapes the binary."

**Rationale:** The philosophical thesis (subjects are embodied, self-knowledge is partial, pedagogy shouldn't demand transparency) has strong precedents in Butler, Glissant, disability rhetoric, psychoanalysis. The novelty claim rests on the *combination* of historiographic diagnosis + design principle + pedagogical architecture + Elbow recovery. The assembly is the contribution, not any single piece.

**Alternatives considered:** Presenting as genuinely new philosophical position — rejected because "we've been doing this" pushback from disability rhetoric and post-process scholars would be immediate and legitimate.

## Formulation Candidates / Key Phrasings

1. **[eas]** "The pedagogy that assumes access is the pedagogy that fails most consequentially for the students most different from its designer." — Context: Closing sentence of the defensive design section. Does the most rhetorical work of any single sentence in the draft.

2. **[eas]** "Accountability without transparency." — Context: Distillation of Butler-Glissant synthesis into an operational design principle. Strongest three-word summary of what the EAS means for assessment.

3. **[eas]** "The void on the map is not empty. It marks the limits of the mapper's instruments, not the absence of the mapped." — Context: Bridge from Arnold/information theory to design. The image works because it holds ontological realism (something is there) and epistemic humility (we can't resolve it) simultaneously.

4. **[eas, elbow-recovery]** "The believing game produces texts, not truths about the self." — Context: Elbow reframe section. Clean distinction between production-for-surprise and excavation models.

5. **[eas]** "The EAS removes the access *requirement* while preserving the access *possibility*." — Context: Bridge to Elbow recovery. Captures the non-rejection stance.

6. **[eas]** "Nonnegotiable variance." — Context: The name for the condition of embodied cognition that the EAS designs around. More precise than "opacity" because it specifies that the issue is variability of access, not mere hiddenness.

7. **[eas, disciplinary-history]** "This was a problem of disciplinary reception, not necessarily of the underlying theories." — Context: Diplomatic framing that lets the argument critique the field's practice without claiming Foucault or Butler were misunderstood.

### Source Decisions

**Arnold's "The Buried Life" preferred over Wordsworth/Coleridge** as romantic touchstone because Arnold is already uncertain about access — the reaching matters, the grasping doesn't happen. Elbow is positioned as more optimistic than Arnold; the EAS is positioned as accepting Arnold was closer to right.

**Kolmogorov/Wolfram demoted to illustration, not primary warrant.** Simon's bounded rationality is the primary epistemological ground. Information theory language preserved in "void on the map" image and one bridging sentence, but the technical mapping is not defended. Footnote 8 in the integrated draft makes this demotion explicit.

## Discarded Directions

- **Strong ontological claim ("not discursive all the way down")** — Rejected because it triggers an unnecessary fight with poststructuralism and new materialism. Replaced with: "materially constrained and variably capacitated; those constraints are not fully captured by discourse description." The weaker phrasing holds the essential ontological commitment without inviting the strongest possible pushback.

- **Kolmogorov complexity as primary warrant** — Rejected as primary because the step from formal systems to cognitive self-modeling is a category mapping that would need to be defended technically. Preserved as: one-sentence illustration ("what computational irreducibility formalizes... the romantic tradition felt as longing") and footnote noting the demotion.

- **Stuckey's *Violence of Literacy* and Hoggart's *Uses of Literacy*** — Rejected from the Butler-Glissant section as tangential anchoring. The argument stands on Butler and Glissant without needing composition-canon signaling at that point. Preserved for: possible footnote or different section.

- **Strong version of address claim ("any encounter with writing is a form of address that solicits a certain kind of self")** — Rejected because it opens a Levinas/Derrida front that can't close in this article. Preserved: the weak version (assignments as solicitations of normed selves) does the needed work.

- **Extended Marx apparatus in the EAS section** — Deferred, not rejected. The labor-capital transformation material is important for the article's overall theoretical apparatus but doesn't need to be resolved for the EAS to function. Lives in marx_and_eas.md lines 205-217 for future development.
