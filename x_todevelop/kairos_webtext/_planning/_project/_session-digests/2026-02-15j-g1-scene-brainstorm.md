## Theoretical Breakthroughs / Key Developments

### G1's Core Irony: The System He Already Has

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): fiction-grant, scene-g1, writing-like-development
- Type: structural
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: this digest

**Discovery:** G1's dramatic engine is not "student learns to set up infrastructure" but "student who operates a professional information system refuses to recognize the course is asking him to build the same thing for his writing." The library circulation desk IS the six-moves framework operating on other people's texts: Grant classifies (by call number), routes (to shelves, holds, ILL), captures (scanning returns), and even uses a file naming convention (call numbers). The course's Week 2 tech setup (folders, index cards, Eli Review, file naming) asks him to build for his own writing what he does for others' writing eight hours a week.

**Implications:**
- The scene should show Grant's library competence concretely and materially — the reader needs to SEE the parallel before Grant does
- This sets up G2's "I do this all day" recognition: G1 must establish library routines specifically enough that the G2 payoff registers
- This sets up G3's infrastructure-recognition: G1 must show Grant setting up the course system with enough skepticism that later payoff surprises
- The frag-02 (writing-like-development) question — "is this appropriation, translation, or recognition?" — gets dramatized experientially in G1 without being answered

### Post-Holiday Returns as Material Opening

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): fiction-grant, scene-g1, textual-accumulation
- Type: structural
- Dashboard-worthy: no
- Captured in: this digest

**Discovery:** Placing G1 immediately after Labor Day gives a material opening: the book drop is full because students dumped returns before the weekend and nobody processed them during the holiday. Grant walks into a full returns cart. This is accumulation without circulation — the exact condition M1 creates for writing (generate freely, sort later). The backlog also gives him something to process competently before he opens the laptop and freezes, making the competence/paralysis juxtaposition concrete.

## Architectural Decisions / Structural Changes

### Narration Mode: Third-Person Restricted Internal (Both Tracks)

**Decision:** Both Grant and Vera scenes use third-person restricted internal narration, NOT first person. The reader gets their thoughts and flavor ("we get their thoughts and flavor but without the 'I'").

**Rationale:** Michael's correction. The dr_green_virens PDF confirms Vera's voice — abstract rhymes, curious wordplay descriptions she narrates to herself ("memory palace database sestina / Mad-Libs red-string murder wall," "thumpity-tripleting gait"). Grant must be palpably different from Vera's register.

**Implications:**
- Grant's internal monologue should NOT mirror Vera's playful-abstract register
- Filipino-American voice register achieved through untranslated Tagalog words surfacing naturally in internal thought (naku, diskarte, anak, lola) — narrator does not gloss them
- Casual but not slangy; increasingly precise vocabulary across the arc
- Vera's narration style = compound-abstract-playful; Grant's = concrete-immediate-resistant

### Reference Librarian as Planted Character

**Decision:** Introduce a Reference Librarian at the circulation desk as Grant's semi-supervisor. This character will later be someone Vera chats with (connecting the two narrative tracks through a shared institutional figure).

**Rationale:** Creates a naturalistic bridge between Grant and Vera tracks without either character knowing they're connected through this figure. The librarian can observe Grant's competence and report it to Vera, or Vera can learn about Grant's library work through the librarian, creating dramatic irony.

**Implications:**
- The librarian needs a light touch in G1 — brief interaction, not a full character
- The librarian's later conversation with Vera creates a V2 or V3 possibility
- Name the librarian? Or leave unnamed in G1? (Open question)

## Formulation Candidates / Key Phrasings

1. **[scene-g1/ending]** The Reference Librarian passes: "You're good at that." Grant doesn't register the compliment. — The deflection IS the scene's closing gesture; the competence acknowledged and refused.

2. **[scene-g1/voice]** "Naku" for mild exasperation (his mother's go-to, now his). — First Tagalog word in the text, deployed without gloss, readable from context.

3. **[scene-g1/voice]** "Diskarte" — Filipino concept of resourceful improvisation, making do, finding the workaround. What Grant does at the circ desk. What the course would call "capture." — Potential for deployment but needs careful placement; could be too loaded for G1.

4. **[scene-g1/backstory]** "Ay, anak, the bus is only forty dollars." But forty dollars is four hours at the desk. — Lola or mother's voice surfacing in Grant's thought; first-gen economics communicated through internal monologue, not exposition.

5. **[scene-g1/structural]** The scene's work is to make the library-course parallel visible to the reader while keeping it invisible to Grant. — Design principle for drafting.

6. **[scene-g1/accumulation]** The full returns cart after Labor Day = accumulation without circulation. — Material metaphor for M1's pedagogical logic; opens the scene with the course's own conceptual operation happening physically.

## Open Questions

- **Option 1 (misrouted card) — use in G1 or save?** The card accidentally entering the returns pile and being noticed by the Reference Librarian is a strong beat, but may overcrowd G1 if combined with Option 2's competence/paralysis structure. Could work as a quick moment within the scene or could be saved for G2's "I do this all day" realization. Needs exploration in next session. Urgency: medium; Blocking: no; For: G1 drafting

- **Hybrid of Options 2 and 1?** Could the efficient sort → overwhelmed pause → librarian compliment sequence ALSO include the misrouted card? If so, it might replace the librarian's "You're good at that" as the closing beat (card enters wrong system → librarian retrieves it → brief exchange → scene ends). Urgency: medium; Blocking: no; For: G1 drafting

- **Reference Librarian naming and characterization?** How much of this character appears in G1 vs. later scenes? Should the librarian be named? Gender? Any specific characteristics that make Vera's later interaction with them naturalistic? Urgency: low; Blocking: no; For: G1 drafting, V2/V3 planning

- **How much of Vera's Week 1 experience should Grant reference?** V1 covers the envelope exercise from the designer's perspective. G1 should probably NOT re-narrate the same classroom moment from Grant's angle (redundancy risk). Better to let V1 handle in-class and G1 handle out-of-class. But does G1 need any flashback to classroom moments? Urgency: medium; Blocking: no; For: G1 drafting

- **Lola's shoeboxes as accumulation backstory?** Lola kept everything — receipts, letters, photos in shoeboxes — Grant thought it was hoarding until he needed his birth certificate for FAFSA and she produced it in thirty seconds. That's textual accumulation. Could plant in G1, pay off in G3 or G5 when Grant recognizes his own system working. Urgency: low; Blocking: no; For: G1 drafting, G3/G5 connections

- **Grant's phone as material constraint in this scene?** Character document names phone addiction as material constraint. How does it function at the circ desk? Is it face-down during work, then grabbed when the laptop freezes him? Is it the escape from the course's demands? Urgency: low; Blocking: no; For: G1 drafting

## Discarded Directions

- **Option 3 (accidental fragment that works)** — Not rejected outright, but deprioritized. The idea that Grant accidentally captures a fragment (complaint about folder naming) in a correctly-executed-but-wrongly-named file is conceptually elegant ("YOLO up works even against resistance") but may be too early for Week 2. Grant's resistance is more authentic as paralysis than as oppositional production at this point. The accidental-competence beat works better at G3 ("What You're Automating") when there's enough accumulated material for the surprise to register. Preserved for possible G3 deployment.

- **First-person narration for Grant** — Corrected by Michael. Both tracks use third-person restricted internal. The "I" voice was never the plan; documentation was imprecise. Vera's dr_green_virens PDF confirms third-person for her track.
