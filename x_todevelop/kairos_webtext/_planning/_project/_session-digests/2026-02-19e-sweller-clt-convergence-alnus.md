## Theoretical Breakthroughs / Key Developments

### 1. Disciplinary Non-Conversation as Evidence

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): alnus-constraint-sequencing, edwards-2014
- Type: evidentiary
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: `_alnus.md` convergence section

**Discovery:** Two traditions — cognitive load theory (experimental psychology) and composition process pedagogy (phenomenological/practitioner inquiry) — independently arrived at convergent conclusions about premature task complexity and its effect on learning, then never spoke to each other. The gap is not accidental but structural: composition's social turn (late 1980s) occurred simultaneously with CLT's foundational period, closing the disciplinary channels through which Sweller's work might have reached composition scholars.

**Implications:**
- Constraint sequencing can claim convergent validation from two independent traditions — experimental and pedagogical — that arrived at the same finding through different methods
- The convergence strengthens constraint sequencing's theoretical warrant: it is not a local intuition but a finding robust enough to emerge independently across disciplinary boundaries
- The gap itself is evidence for Edwards 2014's economic analysis of disciplinary reward structures

**Evidence/Support:**
- Sweller 1988: ~8,400 citations on Semantic Scholar; 2019 retrospective characterizes CLT as among most influential theories in educational psychology
- Zero substantive engagement in CCC, CE, Composition Studies, WPA, or WAC journals
- Reid (2021 JOGLTEP): notes cognitive dimensions "resisted by many scholars in composition studies over the past few decades"
- Rifenburg/Portanova/Roen (2021): defensive framing ("still have great influence") implicitly acknowledges departure

**Next steps:** Bookends import of Sweller cluster; confirm Rifenburg/Portanova/Roen characterization via direct reading; check whether any of the collection's chapters engage CLT specifically

### 2. Sweller = (Flower & Hayes) + Salvatori

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): alnus-constraint-sequencing
- Type: conceptual
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: `_alnus.md` convergence section

**Discovery:** Sweller's role in the Alnus argument is precisely characterized as the fusion of two composition-studies contributions: Flower and Hayes provide the cognitive architecture (working memory limitations, competing demands during writing), while Salvatori provides the pedagogical sequencing principle (staged encounters with difficulty). Sweller provides the mechanistic explanation for why both work (cognitive load management determines schema acquisition) and the massive empirical base confirming it.

**Implications:**
- Clarifies Sweller's argumentative function: he is not replacing composition scholars but providing the experimental confirmation and mechanistic explanation for insights they generated through different methods
- Positions VIRENS constraint sequencing as operationalizing convergent findings rather than importing external theory

### 3. The Independent Discovery Roster

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): alnus-constraint-sequencing
- Type: evidentiary
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: `_alnus.md` convergence section

**Discovery:** Five composition scholars independently discovered CLT's core insight through different methods:
- **Perl** (1980): felt sense and premature closure = extraneous load from premature means-ends analysis
- **Rose** (1984): rigid rules creating cognitive bottlenecks = means-ends analysis consuming schema-acquisition resources (most Sweller-adjacent; predates 1988 article)
- **Flower** (1994 and beyond): task representation and competing cognitive demands = problem framing affecting resource allocation
- **Salvatori** (1996): staged encounters with difficulty = managed intrinsic load
- **Elbow** (1973): separating generating from evaluating = distributing cognitive load across temporal phases

**Implications:**
- Each scholar provides a different vocabulary and methodological approach for the same core insight, enriching the Alnus page's theoretical register
- The five together constitute a pattern that cannot be dismissed as coincidence — convergent evolution across methods
