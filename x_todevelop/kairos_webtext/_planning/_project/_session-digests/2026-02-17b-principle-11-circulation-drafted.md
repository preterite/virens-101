## Theoretical Breakthroughs / Key Developments

### Circulation as Multi-Scale with Diverse Appropriation as Consequence

**Metadata:**
- Domain/Thread(s): principle-11, circulation, diverse-economies, appropriation
- Type: conceptual
- Dashboard-worthy: yes
- Captured in: trees_principles.md

**Discovery/Development:** The #11 claim was built through three converging moves: (1) extending Trimbur's insight that circulation shapes writing at every stage to a *scale* claim — circulation operates microeconomically (within drafts, between writer and peer) as well as macroeconomically (disciplinary publication, institutional exchange); (2) synthesizing Barlow (information exists only in motion) with Babbage (mill and store are co-dependent) to establish that writing's inscriptive durability is what makes circulation *traceable* rather than merely present; (3) deploying Gibson-Graham's diverse economies framework to show that traceability reveals *diverse forms of appropriation* (not monolithic capitalist extraction), which is the political consequence. The claim leads with the writer's action (tracing), identifies the consequence (deliberate participation in appropriation), and specifies the scope (whenever texts move — temporal, not spatial framing).

**Key analytical moves:**
- The classroom is not a special economic zone: writers do in the classroom what they do elsewhere, so microeconomic circulation is not pedagogically bounded
- The denaturalization: composition studies is accustomed to seeing macroeconomic circulation as always outside the classroom; #11 argues it happens inside too
- "Accumulation without circulation is hoarding, circulation without accumulation is dissipation" — the Babbage dialectic makes #11 structurally necessary rather than additive, resolving the apparent tension between #3 (accumulate) and #11 (circulate)
- Self-appropriation leads the five-form enumeration because it's the most disruptive for composition studies — students can be the appropriators of their own labor, not just the objects of institutional extraction

**Implications:**
- The Illicium tree narrative (position 10 in tree sequence) may need revision to carry the durability-traceability argument and diverse-appropriation enumeration
- The Olea tree narrative (position 11, appropriation and value) now has #11's Gibson-Graham framing to work with explicitly
- The bridge to #12 is now in place: "under what conditions future production remains possible" hands off to decomposition as the mechanism that keeps value available for re-appropriation

## Architectural Decisions / Structural Changes

### #11 Claim Written in General-Writerly Register, Not Pedagogical

**Decision:** Frame #11's claim and elaboration in terms of what *writers* do, not what *courses* design for. The pedagogical consequence exists but is bracketed for the nexus page and tree narrative rather than foregrounded in the claim block.

**Rationale:** Michael's overarching argument is that the classroom is not economically special — the same diverse forms of appropriation operate wherever writers work. Leading with pedagogy would undercut this claim by implying circulation analysis is a classroom technique rather than a general feature of writing's economic character.

**Implications for:**
- The Illicium nexus page: this is where the pedagogical consequence gets bolted back on — course design that builds circulation into every stage (peer exchange, fragment routing, portfolio decomposition-and-recombination) enacts the principle
- Consistency: follows the same pattern as other locked claims (#1, #3, #5, #9, #10) that state general principles and let tree narratives/nexus pages do pedagogical application

### Summary Paragraph Updated for #11 Alignment

**Decision:** Replaced the summary paragraph's circulation sentences ("Circulation dynamics shape writing at every one of these stages, not only after a text is 'finished.' Any economic analysis of composition must therefore account for who appropriates value from student writing labor—not only through market extraction but through self-appropriation, communal use, and gift.") with a compressed version reflecting the new claim: "Tracing the circulation of textual value through these stages helps writers identify and deliberately participate in the diverse forms of appropriation—self-directed, gift, communal, institutional, market—that occur whenever texts move."

**Rationale:** The old formulation used "student writing labor" (pedagogical register) and led with "market extraction" (monolithic framing the claim counters). The new formulation matches the claim's general-writerly register and enumeration order (self-directed first).
