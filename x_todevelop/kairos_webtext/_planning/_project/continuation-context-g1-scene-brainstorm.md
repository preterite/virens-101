## Key Scene Parameters (Established)

**Setting:** Library circulation desk, Week 2 (immediately after Labor Day). Full returns cart from holiday weekend. Laptop and/or phone nearby but out of the way of job duties. Loose supervision.

**Narration:** Third-person restricted internal (NOT first person). Grant's thoughts and flavor without "I."

**Voice register:** Filipino-American internal monologue with untranslated Tagalog words surfacing naturally — "naku" (mild exasperation, mother's word), "diskarte" (resourceful improvisation), "anak" (child, term of endearment), lola (grandmother). Narrator does NOT gloss these. Casual but not slangy. Concretely immediate, resistant. Palpably different from Vera's compound-abstract-playful register (see dr_green_virens.pdf in project knowledge for Vera's voice model).

**Core irony:** Grant already operates a professional classification/routing/capture system at the library. The course is asking him to build the same thing for his writing. The reader should see this parallel; Grant should not.

**Reference Librarian:** Semi-supervisor, planted for later Vera connection. Light touch in G1 — brief interaction, not a full character. Will later chat with Vera (connecting the two narrative tracks).

**Labor Day anchor:** Grant covered a shift so another student worker could go home for the long weekend. The bus was forty dollars; forty dollars is four hours at the desk. Post-holiday returns cart is full — accumulation without circulation, mirroring M1's pedagogical logic.
