## Key Questions to Explore

### Structural
1. **Where does V1 take place?** The outline suggests office or home workspace. The voice model opens at home (breakfast, cats) and moves to the walking commute. Does V1 begin at home (envelope preparation in the morning) and move to campus? Or is it entirely in one location?

2. **When in Week 1 does V1 occur?** The first class session? The night before? The morning of? After class, reflecting? The temporal anchor matters for what Vera is thinking about — anticipation vs. reflection.

3. **How does the critical requirement (Smith→Babbage→Beecher→Bain historical awareness) enter the scene?** This is flagged as a must-show but it's heavy theoretical freight. Does it surface in Vera's thoughts while preparing? In a specific memory or reading she's done? In the envelope predictions themselves?

4. **What's in the envelopes?** The predictions Vera writes and seals — what specifically does she predict about her students? About the course? About constraint sequencing? These become G6's payoff, so V1 needs to plant them without revealing them.

### Voice and Tone
5. **How does Vera's voice differ from her voice model's prose context?** The dr_green_virens PDF describes a typical morning. V1 is the first day of the semester — a charged moment. Is her register more excited, more anxious, more deliberately calm?

6. **How much VIRENS system infrastructure shows in V1?** The voice model is infrastructure-heavy (DEVONthink, Readwise, VIRENS morning brief). Does V1 show this or is it more intimate — Vera with envelopes, pen, and her thoughts?

### Connections
7. **What does V1 plant for V2?** V2 (Weeks 6-7) has Vera noticing "something distinctive" about Grant during BEAM. Does V1 plant Vera's attention to students who surprise her? Her habit of looking for pattern-recognition in student work?

8. **Does V1 need to show Vera in the classroom at all?** Or is it entirely before/after class, with the class itself elided? The envelope exercise is the iconic moment — but is it stronger shown or anticipated?
