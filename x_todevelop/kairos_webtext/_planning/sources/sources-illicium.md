# Illicium Sources: Circulation

Position 10 in the QUANTITATION sequence. Scenes G2, V4, G6.

The Illicium nexus makes three claims that build on each other: writing's inscriptive durability makes circulation traceable; circulation operates at every scale, not only post-publication; and tracing circulation reveals diverse forms of appropriation. The tree narrative establishes the durability-traceability premise that claim #11 then deploys.

---

## Claim 1: Inscriptive Durability Makes Circulation Traceable

**Core formulation:** Circulation can only be tracked because writing produces durable artifacts — texts persist in ways that spoken exchange does not, and that persistence makes the movement of value *traceable* rather than merely present.

**Sources:**

**Charles Babbage**, *On the Economy of Machinery and Manufactures* (1832). Babbage's distinction between the mill (processing) and the store (memory) in computing machinery provides the structural metaphor: accumulation without circulation is hoarding, circulation without accumulation is dissipation. Writing's inscriptive durability is what gives it a store — texts persist as traceable artifacts rather than dissipating like speech. The Babbage connection also links backward to Acer (position 3): technology substitutes capital for labor, and the store is where that capital accumulates.

**John Perry Barlow**, "The Economy of Ideas" (1994). Barlow's claim that information exists only in motion — that its value is constituted by circulation rather than possession — provides the counterweight to durability. The Illicium nexus holds both: texts are durable *and* their value requires movement. Neither durability alone (hoarding) nor motion alone (dissipation) produces value; both are necessary.

**Why this claim comes first:** The durability premise grounds everything that follows. Without durable artifacts, circulation would be present but untraceable, and the analytical tools the webtext offers (operational vocabulary, citation as dependency) would have nothing to work on. This is the enabling condition for the entire third arc of the QUANTITATION sequence.

**Status:** ✅ Sources identified. Babbage available in Project Knowledge context. Barlow available online.

## Claim 2: Circulation Operates at Every Scale

**Core formulation:** Following John Trimbur, circulation is not merely what happens after writing but a dynamic that shapes writing at every stage, operating microeconomically (within drafts, between writer and peer) as well as macroeconomically (across disciplinary publication and institutional exchange).

**Source:** Trimbur, John. "Composition and the Circulation of Writing." *College Composition and Communication* 52.2 (2000): 188-219.

**What Trimbur does:** Recovers delivery/circulation as the neglected fifth canon, arguing that the production-centered model of composition (invention → arrangement → style → memory → delivery-as-afterthought) obscures how circulation dynamics shape writing at every stage. Connects Marxian production-distribution-exchange-consumption cycle to rhetorical canons.

**What the Illicium nexus does with Trimbur:** Extends the insight in two directions. First, a *scale* extension: Trimbur's examples are primarily macroeconomic (disciplinary publication, institutional writing requirements). The Illicium nexus adds microeconomic circulation — within drafts, between writer and peer, from fragment to fragment within a corpus. Second, a *durability* extension: Trimbur assumes but doesn't foreground that circulation requires traceable artifacts. Pairing Trimbur with Babbage's mill/store makes the enabling condition explicit.

**Supporting:** Horner, Bruce. *Terms of Work for Composition: A Materialist Critique*. SUNY Press, 2000. Horner's materialist emphasis — value isn't just produced, it moves — provides the disciplinary grounding within composition studies for taking circulation seriously as economic rather than merely rhetorical.

**Status:** ✅ Trimbur in foundational sources and Project Knowledge. Horner in foundational sources.

## Claim 3: Tracing Circulation Reveals Diverse Appropriation

**Core formulation:** Tracing the circulation of textual value helps writers identify and deliberately participate in the diverse forms of appropriation that occur whenever texts move.

**Source:** This is claim #11 in the webtext's argumentative sequence — the Illicium nexus *establishes the premise* (durability + scale) that claim #11 *deploys*. The analytical apparatus for diverse appropriation belongs to Olea (position 11), but the Illicium nexus must point toward it.

**Gibson-Graham**, J.K. *A Postcapitalist Politics*. University of Minnesota Press, 2006. Provides the five-form enumeration of appropriation (self-directed, gift, communal, institutional, market) that the claim references. The Illicium nexus names diversity without pre-empting the taxonomy — "diverse channels," "what forms" — leaving the specific enumeration to Olea.

**Edwards (2014)** provides the framing that positions the composition classroom as a site where these diverse appropriation forms are already operating, not as a special economic zone exempt from circulation dynamics.

**Why this claim is a pointer rather than a full development:** The architectural decision (session 2026-02-17c) is that Illicium's job is durability-traceability and Trimbur attribution, not the appropriation typology. Adding the five-form enumeration here would create collision with Olea. Instead, the Illicium nexus closes by opening the question — if durable texts circulate, then value moves, and the question becomes what forms that appropriation takes — handing off to Olea.

**Status:** ✅ Gibson-Graham in foundational sources. Pointer structure decided.

## Scene Grounding

**G2 — Grant's early encounter with circulation.** Circulation is already happening to his work before he notices it. Peer exchange, instructor reading, institutional record-keeping — his texts are moving through channels he didn't design and didn't choose. The scene dramatizes the difference between circulation-as-theoretical-concept and circulation-as-felt-experience.

**V4 — Vera adjusts course design in response to actual circulation patterns.** Material is moving differently than she intended — some fragments circulate productively (reappearing in later work, generating peer response), others stall. Her mid-semester adjustment is a circulation intervention: redesigning the channels, not the content.

**G6 — End-of-term synthesis.** The full arc of circulation becomes retrospectively visible. Grant can now trace where his material went, who touched it, what changed, and what forms of appropriation occurred at each stage. This is the traceability payoff: durability makes retrospective analysis possible.

**Status:** ⬜ Scene drafting pending. G2 ready for brainstorming (overlaps with Ilex infrastructure visibility scene). V4 and G6 TBD.
