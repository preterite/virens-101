# Sources to Add

Sources identified during development that need integration.

## Added 2026-02-19 (Sweller/CLT Convergence for Alnus)

- **Sweller, John**. "Cognitive Load During Problem Solving: Effects on Learning." _Cognitive Science_, vol. 12, no. 2, 1988, pp. 257-285.
  - Foundational CLT article; ~8,400 citations (Semantic Scholar); already in constraint-sequencing background reading; promoted to direct citation for Alnus nexus

- **Sweller, John, Jeroen J. G. van Merriënboer, and Fred Paas**. "Cognitive Architecture and Instructional Design: 20 Years Later." _Educational Psychology Review_, vol. 31, 2019, pp. 261-292.
  - **Durable citation source for CLT impact claims** — retrospective that characterizes 1998 article as "one of the most cited articles in the educational field with currently over 5000 citations"; anchors the citation-count claim in published scholarship rather than live database snapshot

- **Sweller, John, Jeroen J. G. van Merriënboer, and Fred G. W. C. Paas**. "Cognitive Architecture and Instructional Design." _Educational Psychology Review_, vol. 10, no. 3, 1998, pp. 251-296.
  - Full CLT specification; ~5,000+ citations; the theory's canonical elaboration

- **Rose, Mike**. _Writer's Block: The Cognitive Dimension_. Southern Illinois UP, 1984.
  - Most Sweller-adjacent argument in composition; rigid prematurely applied rules create cognitive bottlenecks; pre-dates Sweller 1988 but convergent finding

- **Kellogg, Ronald T.** "Training Writing Skills: A Cognitive Developmental Perspective." _Journal of Writing Research_, vol. 1, no. 1, 2008, pp. 1-26.
  - Bridge figure between CLT and writing studies; explicitly engages cognitive load in writing context

- **Rifenburg, J. Michael, Patricia Portanova, and Duane Roen**, eds. _Pedagogical Perspectives on Cognition and Writing_. Parlor Press, 2021.
  - Most recent comp studies collection on cognition and writing; defensive framing ("still have great influence") implicitly acknowledges field's departure from cognitive approaches; emphasis on metacognition/transfer rather than CLT

- **Reid, Lynn**. "Cognitive Load, Multimodal Composing and Writing Center Support." _JOGLTEP_, vol. 7, no. 1, 2021, pp. 1223-1241.
  - Closest thing to CLT engagement in composition-adjacent scholarship; notes resistance to cognitive dimensions in the field
