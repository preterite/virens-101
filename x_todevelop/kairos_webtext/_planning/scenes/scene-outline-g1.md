### Core Irony

Grant already operates a professional classification/routing/capture system at the library. The course is asking him to build the same thing for his writing. **The reader should see this parallel; Grant should not.** The scene's work is to make the library-course parallel visible to the reader while keeping it invisible to Grant.

### Beat 1: Processing (Competence + Resentment + Flash)

Opens in media res — Grant already behind the desk, already processing the post-Labor-Day backlog. Returns cart full from the holiday weekend.

**Jess**: Named absent coworker. Grant covered Saturday so Jess could take the bus home for Labor Day. And Sunday. Now Jess texted this morning — some excuse — could Grant cover Tuesday too. Twelve hours she owes him. Forty dollars for the bus, Jess said in August; forty dollars is four hours at the desk. Grant's internal math is economic: accounting, debt, circulation of favors that don't circulate back. First-gen economics as lived calculation, not exposition. Jess is set up for potential return in later scenes; if not, she's texture.

**Spectral weekend flash**: Embedded in the resentment flow, not a separate beat. Working Sunday in the empty library. The way the stacks sounded different with nobody in them — not quiet, exactly, but _listening_. Automated lights clicking on as he walked ranges that hadn't been triggered all weekend, the building waking up around him. "Spectral" is Grant's own sensory word → hyperlink to the bestiary. Two to three sentences. Shows Grant as someone with an attentive inner eye he doesn't value or name. Establishes that the library is a space he inhabits more fully than the classroom — he's here on weekends, holidays, when nobody else is.

**Library routine shown concretely**: RFID pad beep, ILS screen showing item discharged under his login, check screen for flags (hold → holds shelf; ILL return → ILL bin; course reserve → reserves shelf; clean → truck), sort onto truck by call-number range/floor for shelvers. Grant makes classification-and-routing decisions dozens of times per shift without thinking of them as decisions. Interrupted by equipment checkouts (laptops, chargers — shows him interacting with student peers who are studying while he's working). The rhythm of competence on display.

**ILS transaction log** (latent, not narrated): Every transaction logs Grant's staff ID and timestamp. The system captures his labor. Nobody checks. The surveillance infrastructure exists and is already counting him — but passively. **Do not make this explicit in G1.** Let the ILS screen be visible; the reader will feel it when the skepticism arc arrives in G3.

### Beat 2: Paralysis (Laptop + Shoeboxes)

Grant turns from the ILS terminal to his laptop. Two screens side by side. LMS page: set up folder structure, file naming convention, index cards, Eli Review account. The course is asking him to build for his own writing what he just operated for others' writing.

**Shoeboxes**: Involuntary association when he sees "folder structure." Lola's shoeboxes under the bed — receipts, letters, photos. Grant thought it was hoarding until he needed his birth certificate for FAFSA and she produced it in thirty seconds. The thought surfaces and is pushed away: _that's different, that's just stuff._ The dismissal plants for G3/G5 recognition. Two to three sentences. It should feel involuntary — the association surfacing and being refused.

Then the freeze. The course instructions sit on screen. Grant doesn't act.

---

### Beat 3: Deflection (Phone)

Close laptop. Pick up phone. The phone is where Grant goes when the course asks too much — the escape valve. Brief.

---

### Beat 4: Acknowledgment (Librarian)

Reference Librarian emerges from back offices, passes through the desk area. Not checking on Grant — just passing through. Notices what's on the trucks.

The librarian's line should be **specific to the sorting operation** — commenting on Grant's routing logic in terms the reader can map to the six-moves framework without Grant or the librarian knowing that's what they're describing. NOT the generic "You're good at that" — something that names the _classification_ competence specifically enough to sharpen the dramatic irony.

"Homework?" "Nah, just... organizing stuff." The librarian's line. Grant doesn't register the compliment. Scene ends.

**Reference Librarian**: Semi-supervisor. Light touch in G1 — brief interaction, not a full character. Planted for later connection to Vera's track (librarian will chat with Vera in V2 or V3, connecting the two narrative tracks through a shared institutional figure without either narrator knowing). Name and characterization deferred; can remain unnamed in G1 if cleaner.
