# Fragment: Essay as Node, Not Telos

## Core

The essay is valuable not as finished product nor as the goal toward which process aims, but as a _stage_ in accumulation—what you can do next things with.

## Elaboration

This distinguishes the position from both product-focused pedagogy and process pedagogy. Process pedagogy still treated the essay as telos; we valued messy drafting because it produced better final texts. Constraint structure treats the essay as a node in a system of texts and practices, a platform for further work. This connects to the economy framing: the essay isn't consumption (endpoint) but production (input to future cycles). The article could develop this by showing how the course's accumulation arc operationalizes this—how texts from early weeks become resources for later weeks, how nothing is "finished" in the disposable sense. The challenge is distinguishing this from "portfolio pedagogy," which also values collection but often still treats the portfolio as summative product.
