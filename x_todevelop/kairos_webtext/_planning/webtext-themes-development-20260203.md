## 1. Origin Point: The 2016 Pedagogy Passage

The brainstorming session began with a passage from Michael's 2016 *Pedagogy* article that synthesizes memory, time, technology, and textual circulation:

> Brooke suggests that "memory is a canon that focuses our attention on the relationship between discourse and time." [...] Barlow asserts that information "only really exists in the Delta" or space of change and motion [...] reading is chronos become kairos [...] we might ask students to see their reading and writing as simultaneously in motion and historically situated, drawing attention to its inputs and outputs, and thereby to what it might do or become as it moves through a textual cycle of production, distribution, consumption, and re-production.

### Connections identified

The passage contains the seed structure of VIRENS 101's major innovations:

- **Chronos/kairos distinction** maps onto course's temporal architecture: syllabus operates in chronos (scheduled constraints), but pedagogical goal is kairotic transformation through accumulation.
- **Barlow's "information in motion"** is the principle behind textual accumulation — fragments must circulate (peer review, portfolio revision) to exist as intellectual capital.
- **"Textual cycle of production, distribution, consumption, and re-production"** is the Value Appropriation Cycle the course makes visible to students. Johnson-Eilola's "intertextuality with teeth" describes what happens when accumulated fragments begin responding to each other.
- **"Diverse economy"** (from Gibson-Graham [[@gibson-graham2006postc]] via the passage's final gesture) names the course's treatment of writing as simultaneously use-value and exchange-value, labor and capital.

### Provocations generated

- The passage already holds Barlow in tension with access questions — information "has to move between senders and receivers" assumes infrastructure for that movement.
- "Reading is chronos become kairos" implies a conversion event. Bergson and Heidegger [[@heidegger1977quest]] would challenge this as spatializing continuous temporal experience. The accumulation model is philosophically closer to durée than to modal switching.
- The "diverse economy" reference opens J.K. Gibson-Graham's framework but can't develop it here — belongs in the book, not this article.

## 2. The Christen-Barlow Collision

### Barlow as fraught citation

Barlow's "The Economy of Ideas" (1994) is the passage's source for information-in-motion. But Barlow is also the author of "A Declaration of the Independence of Cyberspace" (1996), which asserts that information "wants to be free" — a position now understood as naive at best, dangerous at worst. The passage uses Barlow instrumentally (information must move to exist) without endorsing his politics of openness. The article needs to name this tension explicitly.

### Christen as corrective

Kimberly Christen's [[@christen2012does]] "Does Information Really Want to Be Free?" (2012) provides the direct polemic response. Christen argues from indigenous knowledge management that some information is culturally constrained — access should be context-specific, not universally open. Her concrete contribution: proposing machine-readable cultural protocols (analogous to robots.txt but for indigenous knowledge governance). This maps directly onto the course's permission flags model: information access is not binary (open/closed) but graduated and context-dependent.

**Key formulation**: robots.txt is not a technical enforcement mechanism but a *voluntary compliance convention* — it works because legitimate actors choose to honor it. Course's permission flags operate identically: students learn that information circulation depends on social agreements about appropriate access, not on locks.

### Doctorow as third position

Cory Doctorow's platform capitalism critique adds a third vector: neither Barlow's utopian openness nor simple restriction, but the recognition that platform infrastructure captures value from information circulation regardless of creator intent. Students producing text on institutional platforms (Canvas, OneDrive) are already inside this dynamic. The course makes it visible rather than pretending it doesn't exist.

### Pedagogical sequencing

The Barlow-Christen-Doctorow sequence could structure a unit within the course or serve as the article's theoretical backbone:

1. **Barlow**: information in motion (Movement 1-2 intuition — generate, circulate)
2. **Christen**: information under constraint (Movement 3 recognition — not all circulation is beneficial)
3. **Doctorow**: infrastructure captures value from circulation (Movement 4 synthesis — understanding the systems you're inside)

### Decision for article

Christen confirmed as key interlocutor. Top of list for Bookends/DEVONthink import. Barlow acknowledged as useful but historically situated. Doctorow available if needed but may be scope creep.

## 3. The Clickstream Fiction as Genealogical Artifact

### Summary

"In the Clickstream" (2007) is a serial short fiction published on Michael's weblog, set at West Point "twenty minutes into the future." Protagonist Dr. Edwards teaches "Database Composition" to cadets. Cadet Mala Casey faces honor board investigation after her thesis prospectus arguing LTTE's open information distribution strategy goes viral via clickstream. CTC (Combating Terrorism Center) flags her work. Edwards advises her to write a second essay — a personal/reflective piece about composing the first essay, managing uncertainty and affect. This meta-essay goes viral (1000+ spingbacks). Thesis approved. Casey branches Signal Corps, gets engaged to Tamil partner from Sri Lanka.

### Structural correspondences to VIRENS 101

The fiction contains the course's major innovations in narrative form, eighteen years before their pedagogical formalization:

| Fiction element | VIRENS 101 analogue |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Database composition | Textual accumulation as institutional method |
| Clickstream/spingbacks | Circulation-tracking making Value Appropriation Cycle visible |
| Mala's dual response (formal argument + personal essay) | Content/reasons dual-track structure |
| Edwards: "reflective writing can be a tool for managing information, a tool for managing affect" | Metacognitive process documentation layer |
| Edwards' advice to proliferate then manage proliferation | YOLO up, constrain down |
| Constraint sequencing as survival strategy | Constraint sequencing as pedagogical method |
| "Altruism meets military intelligence via data mining" | Christen's question before reading Christen |

### The Christen connection performed

The fiction's plot *is* the Christen critique performed as narrative. Barlow-position infrastructure (open circulation) is instrumentalized for intelligence gathering. Edwards' counter-strategy is also Barlovian: disclosure, proliferation, making everything public. But the story shows that open information circulation built the surveillance infrastructure that trapped Mala — her high-profile clickstream made her a target. Christen's question (who benefits from openness, who is harmed?) is the plot motor. The story holds Barlow and Christen in unresolved tension.

### Genre trajectory as theoretical work

Fiction (2007) → course design (2024-2025) → scholarly article (forthcoming). Each genre afforded different thinking:

- **Fiction** held ideas in narrative form that couldn't yet be formalized. Tolerated contradictions, ambiguities, unresolved tensions. "Dr. Edwards" as displaced self allowed inhabiting the situation without resolving into thesis.
- **Course design** operationalized the intuitions. Formalized what fiction had explored provisionally.
- **Scholarly article** must now theorize the relationship between the other two.

This trajectory itself enacts the diverse economy of knowledge production the course teaches. Academic fiction published on a weblog isn't "real" scholarship by the academy's Value Appropriation Cycle. Yet it did the intellectual work of generating the framework. The fiction is retrievable now only via Wayback Machine — its survival depends on the same voluntary circulation protocols (robots.txt conventions) the course asks students to examine.

### Decision for article

The clickstream fiction is too substantial for this article — it's its own piece, or a chapter. But the *fact* of the genre trajectory (fiction → course design → article) can be acknowledged in 2-3 sentences as evidence that the ideas developed across genres before achieving pedagogical specification. Salvatori's [[@salvatori1996conve]] "in nuce" provides the theoretical frame: seed form containing the structure of the mature plant.

## 4. Bergson and Heidegger on Temporality

### The objection

A theorist working in Bergson's temporality or Heidegger's primordial temporality would find Brooke's chronos/kairos framing reductive. Both would argue that dividing time into two *types* you switch between has already spatialized continuous temporal experience.

### Bergson's durée

Central concept: *durée* (duration) = lived experience of time as continuous, flowing, interpenetrating. Each moment carries everything that came before *in* it. Analogy: a melody — you don't hear each note as isolated; previous notes remain present as you hear the next one.

**Connection to VIRENS 101**: Bergsonian durée maps onto the textual accumulation model with precision. Fragments don't sit inert in OneDrive, then suddenly convert from stored chronos-material into kairotic insight. They're *present in the student's ongoing intellectual work*, shaping what the student notices in readings, what they write in the next fragment, what connections they recognize in peer review. The accumulation is durée: past work interpenetrating present work as continuous qualitative intensification. The "corpse to compost to garden" arc is a Bergsonian process — not three stages separated by boundaries but continuous transformation where the previous state is always still present in the current one.

**Key formulation**: The fragment model designs conditions for *qualitative intensification within continuous duration*, not conversion from measured time to opportune time. Constraints that arrive on schedule operate in clock-time, but their pedagogical effect is to change the quality of the student's ongoing temporal experience.

### Heidegger's primordial temporality

In *Being and Time*, Heidegger distinguishes "vulgar time" (clock time, sequence of now-points) from "primordial temporality" — the fundamental structure of human existence as always-already three things simultaneously: ahead of itself (projecting into future), thrown (carrying past it didn't choose), and alongside things in present.

**Connection to VIRENS 101**: The dev log's "What I did / What happened / What I learned" operationalizes Heideggerian temporal ecstases for first-year students without requiring them to read Heidegger:
- "What I did" = thrown past (facticity of what occurred)
- "What happened" = present engagement (what showed up in the encounter)
- "What I learned" = projected future (what this makes possible going forward)

### Clickstream connection

The CTC's case against Mala is essentially a Bergsonian crime: they spatialized her continuous intellectual durée into discrete clickstream data points, arranged on a timeline, and inferred causation from sequence. Edwards' counter-strategy: put her intellectual life back into motion through the personal essay that narrates the *flow* of composition rather than the *sequence* of outputs. Duration resists spatialization. That's Bergson's argument, performed as narrative.

### Decision for article

Don't engage Bergson or Heidegger explicitly unless a reviewer pushes. But write about chronos/kairos with appropriate modesty — as heuristic rather than ontology.

**Inoculating sentence**: "The chronos/kairos distinction offers a useful pedagogical vocabulary for temporal experience, while the course's accumulation model suggests that the relationship between measured time and opportune time is less a conversion than a continuous intensification."

One sentence. Inoculates against objection without litigating phenomenological temporality.

## 5. Autobiographical Material: Scope and Discipline

### The institutional trajectory as evidence

Every institutional stop on Michael's trajectory is a different *constraint regime for computation*:

| Institution | Constraint regime | What became visible |
|------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Carnegie Mellon | Computing as invisible abundance | NeXT workstations so ubiquitous they disappeared into background |
| Montgomery College | Computing as scarce resource | Dusty beige terminals, long lines, rationing by queue |
| UMD | Computing as institutional bureaucracy | Mazes, maintenance backlogs, access as navigation problem |
| UMass (with Moran) | Computing as scholarly question | "Who computes?" as researchable inquiry |
| West Point | Computing as surveilled infrastructure | Clickstreams, CTC monitoring, classification protocols |
| Afghanistan | Computing as strategic asset | "Who computes?" with operational/lethal consequences |

Not six different topics. One phenomenon — material access to writing technology — observed under six constraint regimes. The observation that *the same question looks fundamentally different depending on which constraints structure it* is the core insight behind constraint sequencing as pedagogical method.

### GI Bill as methodological warrant

The GI Bill establishes that Michael's own access to intellectual positions he now holds was materially constrained and materially sequenced. Military service structured educational trajectory. GI Bill converted military labor into educational access — specific material transformation with specific institutional mechanism. These are instances of the argument, not biographical details. Material conditions sequenced access to ideas, and the sequence shaped what could be seen.

The $30-35 student tech stack is the design-level consequence: index cards and institutional Office licenses and Zotero chosen not from reading about low-cost pedagogy but from *being* the student standing in line at the beige terminal.

### Villanueva's distinction as editorial discipline

"I do political economy; you do economy." This is the article's self-positioning statement and the sharpest tool for deciding what autobiographical material belongs. The test: does a detail illuminate a *material/economic* relationship (access, cost, infrastructure, constraint), or does it introduce a *political* argument needing its own development?

| Material | Test | Verdict |
|----------|------|---------|
| CMU→Montgomery College→UMD access trajectory | Economy (constraint regimes, material access differences) | **Stays**: 2-3 sentences |
| GI Bill / unfunded first year | Economy (military labor → educational access, material sequencing) | **Stays**: 1-2 sentences |
| Moran at UMass | Economy (scholarly framing of access question) | **Stays**: as scholarly lineage citation |
| "Who computes?" → Afghanistan | Economy (same question under different material conditions) | **Stays**: 2-3 sentences as pivot point |
| Villanueva's greeting | Economy (methodological self-positioning) | **Stays**: one sentence, enormous work |
| Elbow-veteran tension | Political (pacifism vs. military service as intellectual positions) | **Different article** |
| Graham's diverse economies | Political economy (Victor's register, not Michael's) | **Book, not article** |
| Clickstream fiction extended analysis | Own genre, own argument, own scope | **Own article or chapter** |
| Detailed committee genealogy | Inside-baseball | **Goes** |
| Extended Afghanistan treatment | Opens geopolitical arguments that can't close in scope | **Goes** |

**Total autobiographical material by this accounting**: one concentrated paragraph, maybe a page. Every sentence load-bearing.

### Why this helps rather than hurts

Composition studies has a strong tradition of autobiographical scholarship — Villanueva's [[@villanueva2005towar]] *Bootstraps*, Rose's [[@rose1989livet]] *Lives on the Boundary*, hooks's *Teaching to Transgress*, Elbow's confessional mode. The audience expects and values personal disclosure *when it does argumentative work*. Risk is not including personal material but including material that doesn't earn its place. A tightly controlled autobiographical passage that demonstrates the article's argument by enacting it — showing how material constraint structured the very ideas the article proposes — is methodologically coherent, not self-indulgent. The article practicing what it preaches.

**Frame**: one concentrated paragraph, every sentence load-bearing.

**Discipline**: Victor's. Economy, not political economy. Trace the material flows. Let the politics show through the economics. Trust the reader.
